Windpeed vs Latitude


This is a scatter plot showing humidity as a funciton of latitude. Latitude is defined as the angular distance of a place north or south of the earth's equator, or of a celestial object north or south of the celestial equator, usually expressed in degrees and minutes. Looking at the distribution of this plot we can see that areas with high windspeeds are outliers. From this distribution we can see that the average windspeed on earth is between 5 and 10 mph. It should be noted that the southern hemisphere has a more spaced out distribution of windspeeds per latitude, while the northern hemisphere is more clustered closer together
