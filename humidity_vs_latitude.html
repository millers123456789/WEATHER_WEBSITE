Humidity vs Latitude


This is a scatter plot showing humidity as a funciton of latitude. Latitude is defined as the angular distance of a place north or south of the earth's equator, or of a celestial object north or south of the celestial equator, usually expressed in degrees and minutes. Looking at the distribution of this plot we can see that the densest areas of high % humidity occur in the northern hemisphere. While areas of the southern hemisphere experience the same percentage of humidity the frequency of these points is much less than in the northern hemisphere. There is a vast amount of speculation that can be made about the reason for this ranging from human involvement since the northern hemisphere is more densily populated specifically or simply the environmental terrain is more humid north of the equator
