Temperature vs Latitude


This is a scatter plot showing temperature as a funciton of latitude. Latitude is defined as the angular distance of a place north or south of the earth's equator, or of a celestial object north or south of the celestial equator, usually expressed in degrees and minutes. Looking at the distribution of this plot we can see that the temperatures get higher as the latitude gets closer to zero or the equator.Inversely we can also see that the temperature gets lower as the Latitude gets farther from 0. It is coldest farthest away from the equator or at the North and South Poles which is consistent. This scatter plot shows a clearly visible relationship between Latitude or distance from the equator and temperature
